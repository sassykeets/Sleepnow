Step Three · Dream Intention

What Will You Dream Tonight?

Setting a clear intention before sleep is scientifically proven to increase lucid dreaming. This phrase stays with you through the night.

"Tonight I Will Learn Something True About Myself"
Research shows lucid dreaming activates the prefrontal cortex — the seat of self-reflection — allowing access to unconscious patterns unavailable when awake.
Torres-Platas, Northwestern University · Dream Yoga research
"Tonight I Will Let My Heart Begin To Heal"
Clinical neuroscience confirms lucid dreaming reduces anxiety, depression and nightmare distress. Lucidity allows conscious emotional processing that continues healing through REM sleep.
Dresler et al · Clinical Neuroscience of Lucid Dreaming · 2025
"Tonight I Will Find The Answer I Have Been Seeking"
Lucid dreamers consistently report solving real-world problems in sleep. Dream states lower the inhibitions that block creative connection during waking thought.
Barrett · Committee of Sleep · Creative problem solving research
"Tonight I Will Remember That I Am Dreaming"
The classic MILD technique — the most studied lucid dream induction method. Simply holding this intention before sleep significantly increases lucidity rates during REM.
LaBerge · MILD technique · International Lucid Dream Induction Study
"Tonight I Will Practice Being Free"
Research with 528 participants found wish fulfilment is the most common use of lucid dreaming. Pleasurable dream experiences create a measurable positive spill-over into waking life mood.
Lucid Dreaming Survey · Brain Facts · Neuroscience of Lucid Dreaming
"Tonight My Sleep Will Restore Me Completely"
Autosuggestion before sleep is clinically validated. Setting an intention for restoration primes the sleeping brain — REM sleep actively refreshes the prefrontal-amygdala connection governing emotional resilience.
Scientific American · Paller · Sleep Engineering Research · 2024
